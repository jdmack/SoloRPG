# Session Zero

## Campaign

The Rise of a Fixer is a grounded modern crime and investigation campaign focused on the rise of Nick Easton from disgraced ex-convict to professional fixer.

The campaign emphasizes:

* Investigation
* Surveillance
* Problem solving
* Relationships
* Reputation
* Criminal ecosystems
* Consequences

The campaign is intentionally not:

* A spy thriller
* A save-the-world story
* A conspiracy story
* A power fantasy about an unstoppable operator

Information, relationships, and reputation are expected to become more important than violence over time.

---

## Nick Easton

Age: 36

Height: 6'2"

Former military reconnaissance operator.

Nick grew up in Ashford's Northside neighborhood. Gang activity existed around him, but he deliberately avoided that path. At eighteen he left home and joined the military, largely because it represented his best opportunity to escape his circumstances.

Nick served approximately fourteen years before the Abdul Rahim Incident.

---

## Abdul Rahim Incident

Abdul Rahim was an Afghan militia leader and intelligence asset.

Although useful to military operations, Rahim's organization was involved in murder, kidnapping, and sexual violence.

During a meeting with local civilians, Nick witnessed Rahim's men abusing and attempting to seize a young woman.

Nick requested permission to intervene.

Permission was denied.

Twice.

Nick made a deliberate decision.

He lowered his rifle, approached on foot, drew his sidearm, and executed Rahim with a single headshot.

Afterward, he shoved a superior officer during a confrontation.

Nick never denied responsibility.

He was court-martialed, dishonorably discharged, and sentenced to four years in military prison.

---

## Nick's Philosophy

Nick accepts responsibility for his actions.

He does not believe he was wrongly convicted.

His grievance is not the prison sentence itself.

His grievance is the idea that punishment continues indefinitely after a sentence has been served.

A core belief:

"A debt paid should stay paid."

---

## Moral Code

* People who are "in the game" knowingly accept risk.
* Innocent bystanders are off limits.
* Children are off limits.
* Sexual violence is intolerable.
* Torture is unacceptable.
* Reputation matters.
* Promises should be kept.

---

## Marcus Reed

Marcus Reed is Nick's childhood friend.

Both men grew up in Northside.

Both entered the military.

Marcus eventually returned home and built a life in Ashford's gray economy as a connector and problem solver.

Marcus enjoys bourbon, rye, and scotch and has turned liquor into a genuine hobby.

At campaign start he is hesitant to publicly associate with Nick, not because he fears him, but because Nick's reputation is politically and socially toxic.

Over time Marcus is expected to become one of Nick's most important allies.

---

## Ashford

Ashford is a fictional Michigan city with a population of approximately 250,000.

Once a manufacturing hub, it suffered economic decline but never completely collapsed.

The city contains:

* Working-class neighborhoods
* Industrial districts
* Criminal organizations
* Political corruption
* Revitalized commercial areas

Ashford mirrors Nick himself:

Damaged, functional, and trying to find a future.

---

## Campaign Start

Nick has recently been released from military prison.

He returns to Ashford carrying little more than a duffel bag, an old watch, a faded ball cap, and the skills he developed during military service.

He has no active support system.

No meaningful employment.

No established fixer reputation.

The story begins before Nick becomes the man seen in later missions.
