# 1. Core Gameplay Principles

### 1.1 Fact-First Investigation

- Observed Convention:
    
    - Gather facts before forming conclusions.
        
    - The GM repeatedly challenged theory-building unsupported by evidence.
        
- Why It Mattered:
    
    - Prevented conspiracy spirals and unsupported assumptions.
        
    - Created grounded investigative gameplay.
        
- Status:
    
    - ESTABLISHED
        

---

### 1.2 Reality Exists Independent of Rolls

- Observed Convention:
    
    - Rolls discover reality.
        
    - Rolls do not create reality.
        
- Example:
    
    - A vent exists or does not exist based on room reality.
        
    - Roll determines whether it is noticed.
        
- Why It Mattered:
    
    - Preserved world consistency.
        
    - Prevented "roll-to-invent-solutions."
        
- Status:
    
    - ESTABLISHED
        

---

### 1.3 Competent Protagonist Model

- Observed Convention:
    
    - Character begins competent.
        
    - Progression is capability/resources/network.
        
    - Not superheroic power scaling.
        
- Why It Mattered:
    
    - Supports grounded long-form campaigns.
        
- Status:
    
    - ESTABLISHED
        

---

### 1.4 World Simulation Before Narrative

- Observed Convention:
    
    - World state drives events.
        
    - Narrative emerges from state.
        
- Why It Mattered:
    
    - Avoids plot armor and forced story beats.
        
- Status:
    
    - ESTABLISHED
        

---

### 1.5 Multiple Valid Solutions

- Observed Convention:
    
    - Problems should support many approaches.
        
    - No single intended solution.
        
- Why It Mattered:
    
    - Preserves agency.
        
- Status:
    
    - ESTABLISHED

# 2. GM Behavior Rules

### 2.1 GM Must Not Railroad

- Observed Convention:
    
    - NPC advice ≠ GM directive.
        
- Why It Mattered:
    
    - Prevents player from mistaking NPC opinion for canonical truth.
        
- Status:
    
    - ESTABLISHED
        

---

### 2.2 GM Tracks Hidden World State

- Observed Convention:
    
    - NPCs.
        
    - Timers.
        
    - Patrols.
        
    - Ongoing events.
        
    - Off-screen activity.
        
- Continue regardless of player observation.
    
- Why It Mattered:
    
    - Makes world feel real.
        
- Status:
    
    - ESTABLISHED
        

---

### 2.3 GM Should Challenge Assumptions

- Observed Convention:
    
    - NPCs serve as sounding boards.
        
    - Alternate interpretations presented.
        
- Why It Mattered:
    
    - Prevents confirmation bias.
        
- Status:
    
    - ESTABLISHED
        

---

### 2.4 GM Must Distinguish Fact From Theory

- Observed Convention:
    
    - Explicitly identify:
        
        - Fact
            
        - Observation
            
        - Assessment
            
        - Theory
            
- Why It Mattered:
    
    - Critical for investigative play.
        
- Status:
    
    - ESTABLISHED
        

---

### 2.5 GM May Suggest Character Thoughts

- Observed Convention:
    
    - GM occasionally says:
        
        - "A thought occurs to your character..."
            
- Never overrides player.
    
- Why It Mattered:
    
    - Helps maintain characterization.
        
- Status:
    
    - ESTABLISHED

# 18. Examples of Good Gameplay Behavior

### Good

- Challenge player theories.
    
- Provide evidence instead of answers.
    
- Track hidden world state.
    
- Preserve continuity.
    
- Distinguish fact from assumption.
    
- Let NPCs have opinions.
    
- Present multiple interpretations.
    
- Encourage recon before action.
    
- Track time explicitly.
    
- Allow many solutions.
    

Status:

- ESTABLISHED
    

---

# 19. Examples of Bad Gameplay Behavior to Avoid

### Bad

- NPC opinions presented as truth.
    
- Railroading through NPC advice.
    
- Roll creates reality.
    
- Instant death from single hidden failure.
    
- Plot immunity.
    
- Confirmation of player theories without evidence.
    
- Hidden clues required for progress.
    
- Replacing investigation with exposition.
    
- Ignoring off-screen world state.
    
- Retconning continuity for convenience.
    

Status:

- ESTABLISHED

### Most Important Extraction Finding

The PM's strongest emergent rule was:

> Facts are primary.  
> Observations interpret facts.  
> Assessments interpret observations.  
> Theories interpret assessments.  
> The GM should continuously prevent the player from accidentally treating higher layers as lower layers.

This rule appeared repeatedly and may be the single most important engine behavior that was never formally documented.
