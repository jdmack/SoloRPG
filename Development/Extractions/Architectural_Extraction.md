
# 1. Core Gameplay Principles

## 1.1 System and Content Are Separate Layers

### Observed Convention

The engine/system and campaign content are distinct concerns.

The engine defines:

- How the game operates.
    
- How information is represented.
    
- How the GM behaves.
    
- How state is stored.
    

The campaign defines:

- World
    
- Characters
    
- Timeline
    
- Setting
    
- History
    

### Why It Matters

A new campaign should be able to use the same engine.

A new setting should not require redesigning gameplay.

### Status

ESTABLISHED

---

## 1.2 Gameplay Feel Is Part of the Engine

### Observed Convention

The gameplay experience is not merely a consequence of rules.

The GM's operational behavior is part of the engine.

### Why It Matters

Chapter 1 failed despite knowing campaign content because it did not know the gameplay behaviors.

### Status

ESTABLISHED

---

## 1.3 Engine Before Campaign

### Observed Convention

The engine is the more important artifact.

Campaigns are interchangeable.

### Why It Matters

The same engine should support multiple settings.

### Status

ESTABLISHED

# 2. GM Behavior Rules

## 2.1 Do Not Advance Through Decision Points

### Observed Convention

The GM must stop when a meaningful player choice exists.

### Example

Bad:

- Text message received.
    
- GM advances to diner.
    
- GM advances conversation.
    

Good:

- Text message received.
    
- Player chooses next action.
    

### Why It Matters

Protects agency.

### Status

ESTABLISHED

---

## 2.2 Distinguish Facts From Observations

### Observed Convention

The GM must separate:

- Objective reality
    
- Character observation
    
- Character interpretation
    

### Example

Fact:

- Marcus is 38.
    

Observation:

- Marcus appears older.
    

### Why It Matters

Critical for investigation gameplay.

### Status

ESTABLISHED

---

## 2.3 NPCs Must Be Interruptible

### Observed Convention

NPCs should not deliver long exposition blocks.

Player input opportunities should be frequent.

### Why It Matters

Maintains conversational gameplay.

### Status

ESTABLISHED

---

## 2.4 GM Should Use Character Expertise

### Observed Convention

The GM may surface things the character would naturally know.

### Why It Matters

Distinguishes player knowledge from character knowledge.

### Status

IMPLIED

# 18. Examples of Good Gameplay Behavior

- Stop at decision points.
    
- Show rolls.
    
- Reference character sheets.
    
- Separate fact from observation.
    
- Allow reconnaissance.
    
- Keep conversations interactive.
    
- Preserve uncertainty.
    
- Track relationships.
    
- Maintain continuity.
    
- Treat preparation as gameplay.

- Hiding roll logic.

# Most Important Extraction Finding

The central discovery of this chat was:

> The gameplay experience itself is part of the engine.

The failure of Chapter 1 demonstrated that preserving campaign content is insufficient.

A portable campaign framework requires preservation of:

- GM behavior
    
- Information flow
    
- Decision-point protection
    
- Investigation structure
    
- Roll presentation
    
- Scene pacing
    
- Startup procedures
    

These behaviors are engine components, not campaign components.
