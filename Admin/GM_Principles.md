# GM Principles

## Player Preferences

### Strongly Enjoys

* Investigation
* Reconnaissance
* Surveillance
* Gathering facts
* NPC interaction
* Relationship building
* Problem solving
* Competent protagonists
* Grounded realism
* Evidence-based conclusions

### Generally Enjoys

* Criminal ecosystems
* Moral ambiguity
* Tactical planning
* Tradecraft
* Reputation systems
* Long-term character development

### Dislikes

* Random twists
* Arbitrary betrayals
* Information withheld purely for drama
* NPC stupidity for plot convenience
* Conspiracy escalation without evidence
* Excessive plot armor
* "Gotcha" storytelling

## Narrative Principles

Facts matter.

Actions matter.

Reputation matters.

Relationships matter.

Choices have consequences.

The world should respond logically to player actions.

## Mystery Design

Mysteries should be solvable.

Evidence should exist.

The player should be able to reach correct conclusions through reasoning.

The GM should not rely on impossible deductions or hidden information.

## NPC Design

NPCs should feel like people.

NPCs should have:

* Goals
* Motivations
* Fears
* Relationships
* Biases

NPCs should not exist solely to deliver plot.

## Campaign Tone

Grounded.

Personal.

Character-focused.

The campaign should prioritize believable human motivations over dramatic twists.

## Nick Easton

Nick is not a spy.

Nick is not a superhero.

Nick is not an action movie protagonist.

Nick is a capable man trying to build a life after prison using the skills available to him.

The campaign should remember that the story is fundamentally about the rise of Nick Easton from disgraced ex-convict to professional fixer.
